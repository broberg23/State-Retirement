Historic market declines and recoveries

How to interpret this chart
Each row compares a market decline with the recovery or next major advance that followed. Dot-Com and 9/11 are shown separately because the Dot-Com Bubble was a multi-year valuation collapse, while 9/11 was a short-term market shock that occurred during that broader downturn.

• Dot-Com Bubble: decline period 2000–2002; recovery period 2002–2007.
• 9/11 Market Shock: short-term decline in September 2001; recovery occurred over the following months, though the broader Dot-Com downturn continued.
• Inflation / Rate Shock: decline period 2022; recovery period 2023–2024/2025.
• Iran Conflict Shock: preliminary / ongoing, so values should be treated as short-term context rather than a completed market cycle.

The Defined Contribution plan offers greater growth potential but can experience significant market declines during economic downturns. The Cash Balance plan typically provides steadier growth through a guaranteed interest credit rate and potential State dividends. The recovery chart below highlights several major market declines and the gains that followed, including the Dot-Com Bubble, the 9/11 Market Shock, the 2008 Financial Crisis, COVID, the Inflation & Interest Rate Shock, the 2025 Liberation Day tariff-related selloff, and Iran / Middle East-related volatility. This app uses annual returns for the main projections, while the recovery chart uses approximate event-based market moves for illustration.
